How your heart works

Your heart is a muscle about the size of your fist. It is a four‑chambered pump that never stops.
🫁 Right sideCollects deoxygenated blood (blue) from the body and pumps it to the lungs to pick up oxygen.
💪 Left sideReceives oxygenated blood (red) from the lungs and pumps it out to the whole body.
  • The two upper chambers are the atria (they receive blood).
  • The two lower chambers are the ventricles (they pump blood out).
  • Valves are one‑way doors that stop blood flowing backwards — the "lub‑dub" sound is valves snapping shut.
